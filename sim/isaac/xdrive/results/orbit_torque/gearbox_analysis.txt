FORTIS X-Drive Gearbox Selection Analysis
==========================================
Date: 2026-04-01
Based on: Orbit torque sweep simulation (1.0-2.0 m/s, 40 lb robot, mu=0.5)

Motor: REV NEO 2.0 Brushless
  Stall torque: 1.4 Nm
  Free speed: 5676 RPM (594.4 rad/s)

MAXPlanetary Efficiency: ~90% per stage

Simulation Torque Requirements (from orbit_torque sweep):
  Mean torque (worst case, 2.0 m/s): 4.4 Nm
  P95 torque (worst case, 2.0 m/s): 14.6 Nm
  P99 torque (worst case, 2.0 m/s): 23.0 Nm
  Peak torque: 42-75 Nm (sub-ms PhysX collision spikes, not realistic)

  Note: Peak spikes are artifacts of the sphere-roller collision model
  in Isaac Sim. Almost all spikes occur on FR wheel. Real AndyMark 8"
  Dualie omni wheels will not produce these transient impulses.

Max wheel speed needed:
  At 1.0 m/s orbit: ~8.1 rad/s (77 RPM)
  At 1.5 m/s orbit: ~12.1 rad/s (116 RPM)
  At 2.0 m/s orbit: ~16.1 rad/s (154 RPM)
  With 50% margin for speed control: 24.2 rad/s (231 RPM) at 2.0 m/s

SELECTED: 25:1 Bundle (5:1 + 5:1, REV-21-2103 x2)
  Available torque: 28.4 Nm
  P95 margin: 1.94x
  P99 margin: 1.23x
  Mean margin: 6.4x
  Max wheel speed: 23.8 rad/s (227 RPM)
  Max practical orbit speed: ~1.5 m/s
  Stages: 2 (90% x 90% = 81% efficiency)

Rationale:
  - Nearly 2x margin on P95 torque provides comfortable overhead
  - 1.23x margin on P99 covers all but the most extreme transient spikes
  - Max speed of ~1.5 m/s is acceptable for reactor inspection operations
  - 2-stage design keeps gearbox compact (vs 3-stage options)
  - 6.4x margin on mean torque ensures thermal headroom for sustained operation
